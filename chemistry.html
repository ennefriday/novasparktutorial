From atomic structure to organic chemistry — 18 syllabus topics organised into five clear modules so reactions finally click into place.

The Full Syllabus

Five Modules. One Clear Path.

Tap any module below to reveal every topic inside it — exactly as structured for JAMB, WAEC, NECO and GCE.

01
Module 1

Foundations of Matter

4 topics

+

Separation of Mixtures & Purification

Pure and impure substances; boiling and melting points; elements, compounds and mixtures; evaporation, distillation, sublimation, filtration, crystallisation and chromatography.

Chemical Combination

Laws of definite, multiple and reciprocal proportions; the mole concept; Avogadro's number; stoichiometry of reactions.

Kinetic Theory of Matter & Gas Laws

Phenomena supporting the kinetic theory; Boyle's, Charles's, Graham's and Dalton's laws; the ideal gas equation.

Atomic Structure & Bonding

Atomic structure and electron configuration; the periodic table and periodicity; electrovalency, covalency and coordinate bonding; shapes of simple molecules.

02
Module 2

Chemistry of the Environment

4 topics

+

Air

Natural gaseous constituents and their proportions; air as a mixture; uses of the noble gases.

Water

Water as a solvent; hard and soft water; treatment of water for town supply; water of crystallisation, efflorescence and deliquescence.

Solubility

Unsaturated, saturated and supersaturated solutions; solubility curves; true solutions vs suspensions and colloids.

Environmental Pollution

Sources and effects of pollutants; air, water and soil pollution; biodegradable vs non-biodegradable pollutants.

03
Module 3

Reactions & Equilibrium

6 topics

+

Acids, Bases & Salts

General properties and indicators; basicity of acids; pH and pOH; acid-base titrations; hydrolysis of salts.

Oxidation & Reduction (Redox)

Oxidation in terms of oxygen/hydrogen and electron transfer; oxidation numbers; balancing redox equations; IUPAC nomenclature.

Electrolysis

Electrolytes and non-electrolytes; Faraday's laws of electrolysis; electrochemical series and cells; corrosion and cathodic protection.

Energy Changes

Enthalpy of physical and chemical changes; entropy as order-disorder; spontaneity of reactions (ΔG).

Rates of Chemical Reaction

Effects of temperature, concentration, surface area and catalysts; reaction rate curves; activation energy.

Chemical Equilibria

Reversible reactions and dynamic equilibrium; Le Chatelier's principle and the equilibrium constant.

04
Module 4

Elements & Compounds

2 topics

+

Non-metals & Their Compounds

Hydrogen; the halogens; oxygen and sulphur; nitrogen and ammonia; carbon and its oxides — preparation, properties, uses and tests.

Metals & Their Compounds

General properties and extraction; alkali and alkaline-earth metals; aluminium, tin and the first transition series; iron, copper and common alloys.

05
Module 5

Organic Chemistry & Industry

2 topics

+

Organic Compounds

Alkanes, alkenes and alkynes; aromatic hydrocarbons; alkanols, alkanals, alkanones and alkanoic acids; alkanoates, amines, carbohydrates, proteins and polymers.

Chemistry & Industry

Types and raw materials of chemical industries; fine vs heavy chemicals; the relevance of biotechnology to industrial processes.
