The Full Syllabus

Six Modules. One Clear Path.

Tap any module below to reveal every topic inside it — exactly as structured for JAMB, WAEC, NECO and GCE.

01
Module 1

Foundations — Measurement & Vectors

2 topics

+

Measurements and Units

Length, area, volume, mass and time; fundamental and derived quantities; dimensions; limitations of experimental measurement; position and displacement.

Scalars and Vectors

Scalar and vector quantities; relative velocity; resolution of vectors into two perpendicular directions, including graphical methods.

02
Module 2

Mechanics

7 topics

+

Motion

Types of motion; relative motion; linear motion and its graphs; projectiles; Newton's laws of motion; motion in a circle; simple harmonic motion.

Gravitational Field

Newton's law of universal gravitation; gravitational potential; acceleration due to gravity; mass vs weight; escape velocity; parking orbit and weightlessness.

Equilibrium of Forces

Equilibrium of coplanar forces; triangle and polygon of forces; Lami's theorem; principles of moments; centre of gravity and stability.

Work, Energy & Power

Forms of energy and its conservation; energy and society; renewable/non-renewable sources; dams; nuclear and solar energy.

Friction

Static and dynamic friction; coefficient of limiting friction; viscosity, terminal velocity and Stoke's law.

Simple Machines

Types of machines; mechanical advantage, velocity ratio and efficiency.

Elasticity

Elastic limit, yield point, breaking point, Hooke's law and Young's modulus; the spring balance; work done in springs and elastic strings.

03
Module 3

Fluids & Thermal Physics

10 topics

+

Pressure

Atmospheric pressure and its measurement; pressure in liquids; transmission of pressure (Pascal's principle).

Liquids at Rest

Density and relative density; upthrust; Archimedes' principle and the law of floatation.

Temperature & Its Measurement

Thermometric properties; calibration; Celsius and Kelvin scales; types of thermometers.

Thermal Expansion

Linear, area and volume expansivity of solids; volume and apparent expansivity of liquids; anomalous expansion of water.

Gas Laws

Boyle's law, Charle's law, Pressure law; absolute zero; general and ideal gas equations; the Van der Waal gas.

Quantity of Heat

Heat capacity and specific heat capacity; method of mixtures, electrical method and Newton's law of cooling.

Change of State

Latent heat; specific latent heats of fusion and vaporisation; effects of pressure and dissolved substances on melting and boiling points.

Vapours

Saturated and unsaturated vapours; saturated vapour pressure; dew point, humidity and hygrometry.

Structure of Matter & Kinetic Theory

Molecular theory — Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, capillarity; assumptions of the kinetic theory.

Heat Transfer

Conduction, convection and radiation; temperature gradient and thermal conductivity; the thermos flask; land and sea breeze.

04
Module 4

Waves, Sound & Light

8 topics

+

Waves

Production and propagation; classification — mechanical/electromagnetic, longitudinal/transverse; reflection, refraction, diffraction, polarization; interference, beats and Doppler effect.

Propagation of Sound

The need for a material medium; speed of sound in solids, liquids and air; echoes and reverberation.

Characteristics of Sound

Noise vs musical notes; quality, pitch, intensity and loudness; harmonics, overtones and resonance in air columns.

Light Energy

Natural and artificial sources; luminous vs non-luminous objects; formation of shadows and eclipses; the pinhole camera.

Reflection of Light

Laws of reflection; image formation by plane, concave and convex mirrors; the mirror formula; linear and angular magnification.

Refraction of Light

Laws of refraction; refractive index; real and apparent depth; critical angle and total internal reflection; glass prisms and lenses.

Optical Instruments

Microscopes, telescopes, cameras and the human eye; power of a lens; near and far points; sight defects and corrections.

Dispersion & EM Spectrum

Dispersion of white light; colour mixing; rainbow formation; sources, wavelengths and uses of the electromagnetic spectrum.

05
Module 5

Electricity & Magnetism

10 topics

+

Electrostatics

Charging by friction, contact and induction; the electroscope; Coulomb's inverse square law; electric field and potential.

Capacitors

Types and functions; parallel plate capacitors; series and parallel arrangements; energy stored in a capacitor.

Electric Cells

The simple voltaic cell and its defects; Daniell and Leclanché cells; lead-acid and Nickel-Iron accumulators; arrangement and efficiency.

Current Electricity

Emf, p.d., internal resistance; Ohm's law, resistivity and conductivity; the metre bridge and potentiometer; Kirchhoff's law.

Electrical Energy & Power

Commercial units of energy and power; power transmission; heating effects; house wiring and fuses.

Magnets & Magnetic Fields

Natural and artificial magnets; making and demagnetising magnets; magnetic field patterns; the earth's magnetic field.

Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor

Fleming's left-hand rule; force between parallel conductors; the d.c. motor; electromagnets; galvanometer conversion.

Electromagnetic Induction

Faraday's and Lenz's laws; a.c./d.c. generators; transformers; inductance and eddy currents.

Simple A.C. Circuits

Peak and r.m.s. values; resistors, capacitors and inductors on a.c.; R-L-C circuits; resonance frequency.

Conduction Through Liquids & Gases

Electrolytes and electrolysis; Faraday's laws of electrolysis; discharge through gases.

06
Module 6

Modern Physics & Electronics

2 topics

+

Elementary Modern Physics — Bohr's Theory

Models of the atom; energy levels and spectra; thermionic and photoelectric emission; x-rays; radioactivity, half-life, fission and fusion; wave-particle duality.

Introductory Electronics

Conductors, semiconductors and insulators; n-type and p-type semiconductors; diodes and transistors; rectification and amplification.
